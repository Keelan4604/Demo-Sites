# Pricing & Pitch Strategy: West Alabama Fence Redesign

## Pricing Recommendation

### Low Price: $1,500

This is the floor — the minimum you should accept if the owner is hesitant but willing to move forward. At this price, you're delivering a polished single-page site with the contact form, deploying it, and walking away. This works if:
- The owner has never spent money on web design before
- You want the portfolio piece and the case study
- You need the cash flow now

You'd still deliver a quality product, but you'd skip extras like custom photography guidance, Google Business Profile optimization, or any post-launch support.

### Target Price: $2,500

This is the sweet spot — what the project is genuinely worth and what a fence company owner should be willing to pay for a site that directly generates leads. At this price, you'd include:
- The full static site (home, services detail pages if they want them, contact form)
- Mobile-optimized, conversion-focused design
- Help with Google Business Profile updates
- Basic form integration (Formspree, Netlify Forms, or similar)
- One round of revisions
- Deployment to Cloudflare Pages with their domain
- A 30-day post-launch support window for minor tweaks

This is a fair deal for both sides. A single residential fence job ($4K–$8K average) that comes through the new site pays for it and then some.

### Stretch Price: $3,500–$4,000

This is what you pitch if the owner seems enthusiastic, understands the value, or mentions wanting "the works." At this price, you'd add:
- Multi-page site (individual service pages for each fence type)
- Portfolio/gallery page with proper project showcases
- Schema markup and basic local SEO optimization
- Help sourcing and optimizing project photos
- Google Analytics setup
- Ongoing hosting management for 12 months
- Priority support for content changes during the first year

This positions you as a reliable partner, not just a one-time vendor. It also creates a path to future recurring revenue if they want ongoing support later.

## Sales Angle

### Lead with the gap.

The pitch is simple: "Your business is great. Your website doesn't show it."

This owner has built a successful company over 28 years. They have hundreds of positive reviews and BBB accreditation. But their website looks like it was built before smartphones mattered. Every day, potential customers are Googling "fence company Tuscaloosa," landing on their site, and choosing a competitor because the site doesn't inspire confidence.

Don't sell design. Sell the leads they're losing.

### Make the ROI obvious.

Frame the conversation around their average job value:
- "If your average fence installation is $5,000, and this new website brings in just one extra job in the first three months, you've already made your money back."
- This is not a speculative investment. It's straightforward: a better website means more people call, more people call means more estimates, more estimates means more jobs.

### Reduce friction.

The owner likely hasn't done this before. Make it easy:
- "I handle everything. You just give me approval on the design, and I deploy it."
- "The new site runs on modern hosting — it costs almost nothing and you'll never have to worry about updates or security."
- "I'll even help you set up the contact form so estimate requests go straight to your phone or email."

## Delivery Model Recommendation

**Build as a static site. Deploy to Cloudflare Pages.**

Why:
- Zero hosting cost for the client (free tier covers this easily)
- No CMS to hack, update, or break
- Blazing fast load times
- Dead simple to deploy and maintain

WordPress is unnecessary for this type of business. They don't need a blog, an admin panel, or e-commerce. They need a site that looks professional, loads fast, and makes it easy to request an estimate.

If the owner later decides they want to manage content themselves, you could always migrate to a simple CMS like Carrd, Squarespace, or even a headless CMS — but honestly, for a fence company, the best site is the one that works and requires zero ongoing attention.

## After the Sale

Once the site is live and the owner is happy, there are natural upsell opportunities:
- Seasonal landing pages (spring fence season, storm damage repair)
- Google Ads management (fence companies convert well on local search ads)
- Referral to a local photographer for project portfolio shots
- Ongoing retainer for quarterly site updates ($100–$200/quarter)

Don't pitch these upfront. Deliver the site, let the results speak, and circle back in 60–90 days.
