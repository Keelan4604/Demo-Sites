## Why This Business Was Chosen

### Strong commercial signals:

1. **Established, profitable service business.** 28 years in operation means this isn't a startup — they have cash flow, repeat customers, and a working business model. They can afford a website investment.

2. **High-ticket services.** Residential fencing projects typically range from $3,000 to $15,000+. Commercial and agricultural work can be higher. Even a modest increase in lead volume from a better website could easily pay for itself within the first project.

3. **Terrible website, good business.** Their current site is visually stuck in the early 2010s, but the business itself has strong fundamentals — 4.4 stars, 159 reviews, BBB accreditation, nearly three decades of history. This gap between business quality and web presence is the ideal sales opportunity.

4. **Perfect fit for a low-maintenance static site.** Fence companies don't need blog posts, product catalogs, e-commerce, or frequent content updates. The site needs to exist, look trustworthy, load fast, and convert visitors into phone calls and estimate requests. Once built, it runs.

5. **Family-owned with a reachable decision-maker.** No corporate red tape. The owner reads the email, makes the call, and writes the check.

6. **AOL email address.** This is a strong indicator that the owner is not tech-savvy and probably hasn't invested in their web presence intentionally. They likely know the site is bad but haven't prioritized fixing it — or don't know where to start.

---

## What's Wrong With the Current Website

### Visual design:

- **Dated aesthetic.** The site looks like it was designed around 2012–2014. Heavy dark red color scheme (#960028), inconsistent typography, and dense layout that doesn't match modern web expectations.
- **Poor visual hierarchy.** Gallery pages use small thumbnails that don't showcase the craftsmanship of their work. For a visual trade business, this is a major miss.
- **Heavy, oppressive color palette.** The dominant dark red feels outdated and doesn't convey the clean, professional, trustworthy impression a homeowner looks for when choosing a contractor.
- **Footer display issue.** The footer has `display: none` CSS, meaning site footer info is literally hidden from visitors.

### Conversion problems:

- **No testimonials anywhere on the site.** This business has 159 reviews and a 4.4-star rating — none of that social proof appears on the website. Visitors who find the site through Google have no reason to trust them beyond the copy.
- **Weak calls to action.** The "Call Today For a Free Estimate" message exists but isn't designed for modern conversion. No contact form visible. No structured estimate request flow.
- **No service descriptions.** The fence type pages are essentially just galleries with thumbnails. There's no descriptive copy explaining what they offer, what materials they use, or why someone should choose them for that specific type of fencing.
- **No clear value proposition above the fold.** A visitor landing on the homepage has to work to understand why this company is worth calling.

### Trust and credibility issues:

- **No certifications or warranties mentioned.** Despite being BBB accredited, this information doesn't appear prominently.
- **No "About Us" depth.** The site mentions being family-owned but provides no owner name, no story, no photos of the team — nothing to humanize the business.
- **AOL email on a business website.** This actively reduces trust for younger homebuyers and commercial decision-makers.

### Technical issues:

- **Outdated CSS patterns.** Heavy reliance on styling typical of early 2010s WordPress themes.
- **Not optimized for mobile.** While nominally responsive, the experience on mobile is clunky — small thumbnails, cramped navigation, and poor touch targets.
- **Likely slow loading.** Gallery pages with unoptimized images and dated CSS patterns typically underperform on PageSpeed.

---

## Why the Redesign Should Improve Business Results

### Direct revenue impact:

1. **More estimate requests.** A prominent, well-designed contact form with fence type selection makes it easier for visitors to reach out. The current site essentially only offers a phone number.

2. **Higher trust = higher close rate.** When a homeowner is comparing 3–4 fence companies, the one with the professional, modern website and visible social proof signals competence. The current site puts them at a disadvantage even though their actual work quality is strong.

3. **Better mobile experience = more calls.** The majority of local service searches happen on mobile phones. A visitor deciding between fence companies at 7 PM on their couch will call the one whose site works well on their phone.

4. **Social proof visibility.** Surfacing their 4.4-star rating and BBB accreditation prominently on every page creates immediate trust. Right now this proof exists on third-party platforms but not on their own site.

5. **Service clarity.** When a homeowner can read a clear description of wood fence options, see quality portfolio images, and immediately request an estimate — that's a shorter path to revenue than making them browse tiny thumbnail galleries with no context.

### Competitive advantage:

Looking at the Tuscaloosa fence company landscape, most competitors also have mediocre websites. Crimson Fences and T&T Fencing have functional but uninspired sites. A genuinely well-designed site would immediately differentiate West Alabama Fence in the local market.

### Low ongoing cost:

A static site deployed on Cloudflare Pages costs essentially nothing to host. No CMS to maintain, no plugins to update, no security patches. The only ongoing need would be occasional gallery image updates, which are trivial to do.
