## Sending notes

**Before you send, do these three things:**

1. **Deploy the demo** to `westalabamafence.keelanodoherty.org`. The link in the email needs to work — it's the entire pitch.
2. **Fill in your contact details** — replace `[YOUR PHONE]` and `[YOUR EMAIL]` with your actual info.
3. **Read it out loud once.** It should sound like a person, not a marketing template. If any sentence sounds stiff, loosen it up in your own voice.

---

**Why the email is framed this way:**

The "I built something for you without asking — let me explain" opening is intentional. It disarms the weirdness before it can become an objection. You're naming the elephant in the room yourself, which is more credible than just pretending it isn't there. Then you explain the analogy (a contractor's free rendering) which immediately makes it normal and professional.

**Why there's no price in the email:**

Quoting a number cold creates an instant yes/no anchor. If you say $2,500 and they think "too much," the conversation is over before it starts. If you say $800 and they think "that's cheap," you've undercut yourself. Getting them on a call or into a reply thread first lets you understand what they actually want, qualify them, and price accordingly. You're in a much stronger position after one conversation.

**Why the form explanation matters:**

A lot of small business owners don't understand how contact forms work and quietly assume it's complicated or that leads will disappear into the void. Explaining it clearly ("shows up like a regular email, no apps to check") removes a hidden hesitation they probably wouldn't even voice.
